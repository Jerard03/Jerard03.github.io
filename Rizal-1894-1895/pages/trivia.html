RIZAL TRIVIA

THE MISSING PUZZLE PIECES THAT COMPLETE RIZAL'S LEGITIMACY. MYSTERIES AND FACTUAL INFORMATION WOULD BE EXPOSED AND DUG UP HERE.

Dapitan became the living witness to one of the most productive moments of our National hero's life.

Rizal was brought under a maximum security to the steamer S.S. Cebu headed by Capt. Delgras which sailed for Dapitan.

Captain Ricardo Carnicero is the Spanish commandant of Dapitan when Rizal was banished in the city. Rizal eventually wrote a poem entitled ”A Don Ricardo Carnicero” on the occasion of the commandant’s birthday.

Casa Redonda is one room octagonal house which originally functioned as Rizal’s pupil dormitory. It was later on converted into a clinic where Rizal operate the eyes of his patients.

Casa Residencia is the main house of Rizal where his family members stated during their visit. This house encircled with a veranda which is a Japanese inspired.

Rizal’s most significant contribution in the scientific world was his discovery of three species: Draco Rizali which is a flying dragon. Apogonia Rizali is a small beetle. Rhacophorus Rizali is a rare frog.

A piece of land at Sitio Daanlungsod in the Lubugan was bought by Rizal which is now a Municipality of Katipunan.

Rizal wanted to remain in Mindanao and planned to colonize Ponot, located at the Western Coast of the island near Sindangan where he intended to plant coconuts, coffee and cacao.

While Rizal was in Dapitan, he studied nature extensively, researching and collecting specimens of plants, shells, insects, birds, amphibians, and reptiles.

Rizal gathered up to 340 shells representing more than 200 species with the help of his students. This collection is recognized as the richest private collection of Philippine conchology of its time.

European scientists eventually verified a few of the rare animal species Rizal sent, and named them in his honor. A frog was named Rhacophorus Rizali, the new type of beetle was baptized as Apogonia Rizali, Draco Rizali was named to a new species of butterfly, another was named Rizali in the new species of mosquito.

The National Historical Commission of the Philippines (NHCP) mentions a fourth species bearing Rizal’s name. Spathomeles Rizali, a type of fungus beetle, was reportedly one of the many specimens Rizal sent to Germany for identification. It’s also known as the “handsome fungus beetle.”

The official database of Harvard University’s Museum of Comparative Zoology has an entry on Hyrdosyche Rizali. Based on its taxonomy, H. rizali is a type of caddisfly, an insect that starts life as aquatic and becomes terrestrial upon maturity.

Casa Cuadrada is used to accommodate the growing number of Rizal’s pupils in his Talisay School. The area underneath the hut served as workshop of his pupils.
